# About Me I am a professor at the [Universidad Autónoma del Estado de México (UAEMéx)](https://www.uaemex.mx/), a senior consultant at [Scientificomm](https://www.scientificomm.info/), and a **Level 2 member** of [Mexico’s National System of Researchers (SNI), Conacyt](https://conacyt.mx/). With over 20 years of experience, my work focuses on **scholarly communication, Open Science, and Open Access**. My academic background spans **Sociology, Urban Studies, Social Theory, Education, and Data Science and Analytics**. Throughout my career, I have collaborated internationally as a **visiting professor** in *Research Methodology* at the [University of Cuenca, Ecuador](https://www.ucuenca.edu.ec/) and as a **visiting researcher** at the [Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM)](https://www.unam.mx/). Since 2018, I have represented [Figshare](https://figshare.com/) as an **Ambassador for Latin America**, advocating for open-access initiatives. I am also a mentor for [AuthorAID](https://www.authoraid.info/en/), a global network supporting early-career researchers in low- and middle-income countries. Beyond my academic roles, I actively contribute to shaping **Open Science policies**. I played a key role in developing **Mexico’s 2014 Open Access Law** and currently serve on the **Board of Directors** for the [Open Education Conference](https://openeducationconference.org/). I am a member of the **Advisory Group** for the [OA Switchboard](https://www.oaswitchboard.org/) and part of the **Programme Committee** for the [Born-Digital Collections, Archives, and Memory Conference](https://www.bdcam.org/). Additionally, I chair the [Red Iberoamericana de Investigación sobre Globalización y Territorio (RII)](https://riiglobalizacionyterritorio.org/) and am a member of the **Scientific Committee** of the [Red Latinoamericana de Sistemas Sociales y Complejidad (RELASSC)](https://relassc.org/). I firmly believe that **research should be openly accessible** to maximize its societal impact. My work is dedicated to fostering transparency, collaboration, and innovation in **academic publishing and scholarly communication**.
